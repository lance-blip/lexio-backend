# Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 (POPIA) — Key Sections

## Section 9 — Lawfulness of processing
Personal information must be processed lawfully and in a reasonable manner that does not infringe the privacy of the data subject.
> Plain language: Companies can only use your personal information in legal, reasonable ways that respect your privacy.

## Section 11 — Consent, justification and objection
(1) Personal information may only be processed if the data subject consents to the processing.
(3) A data subject may object to the processing of personal information at any time.
> Plain language: Companies need your permission to use your personal data, and you can withdraw that permission at any time.

## Section 14 — Collection directly from data subject
Personal information must be collected directly from the data subject unless exceptions apply.
> Plain language: Companies should get your information directly from you, not from third parties without your knowledge.

## Section 18 — Notification to data subject when collecting personal information
A responsible party must inform the data subject of: the information being collected, the purpose, whether it's voluntary or mandatory, consequences of not providing it, and the data subject's rights.
> Plain language: When a company collects your data, they must tell you what they're collecting, why, and what your rights are.

## Section 19 — Security safeguards
A responsible party must secure the integrity and confidentiality of personal information by taking appropriate, reasonable technical and organisational measures.
> Plain language: Companies must protect your personal data with proper security measures.

## Section 22 — Notification of security compromises (data breaches)
Where there are reasonable grounds to believe that personal information has been accessed or acquired by any unauthorised person, the responsible party must notify the Information Regulator and the data subject.
> Plain language: If your data is breached/leaked, the company MUST tell you and the Information Regulator.

## Section 23 — Right of access
A data subject has the right to request confirmation of whether a responsible party holds their personal information, and to request a record or description of that information.
> Plain language: You can ask any company whether they have your data and request a copy of it.

## Section 24 — Correction and deletion
A data subject may request correction or deletion of personal information that is inaccurate, irrelevant, excessive, out of date, incomplete, misleading, or obtained unlawfully.
> Plain language: You can demand that companies fix or delete your incorrect, outdated, or illegally obtained personal data.

## Complaints
Complaints about POPIA violations can be lodged with the Information Regulator: 010 023 5207 / www.inforegulator.org.za
