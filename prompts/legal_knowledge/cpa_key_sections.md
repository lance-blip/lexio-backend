# Consumer Protection Act 68 of 2008 — Key Sections

## Section 16 — Right to choose
A consumer has the right to select suppliers and to choose or examine goods before purchase.
> Plain language: You can choose who you buy from and inspect goods before buying.

## Section 20 — Right to return goods
(1) A consumer may return goods to a supplier within 6 months if the goods are defective, unsuitable, or unsafe.
(2) The supplier must repair, replace, or refund the goods at the consumer's choice.
> Plain language: If something you bought is broken or defective within 6 months, you can demand a repair, replacement, or refund — YOUR choice, not the shop's.

## Section 22 — Right to information in plain and understandable language
A supplier must provide notices, documents, and visual representations in plain language.
> Plain language: All contracts and notices must be written in language you can understand — not confusing legal jargon.

## Section 41 — False, misleading or deceptive representations
A supplier must not make false or misleading representations about goods or services.
> Plain language: Businesses cannot lie to you about their products or services. False advertising is illegal.

## Section 48 — Unfair terms
A supplier must not offer to supply or enter into an agreement on terms that are unfair, unreasonable, or unjust.
> Plain language: Contract terms that are unfair or one-sided can be challenged and declared void.

## Section 54 — Consumer's right to demand quality service
(1) A consumer may demand that services be performed in a manner and quality that persons are generally entitled to expect.
(2) A consumer may demand that any work be completed within a reasonable time and, if not completed, may cancel without penalty.
> Plain language: You have the right to quality service. If work isn't done properly or on time, you can cancel and get your money back.

## Section 56 — Implied warranty of quality
(1) Every transaction includes an implied warranty that goods are of good quality, in good working order, and free of defects.
(2) Within 6 months of purchase, a consumer can return defective goods for repair, replacement, or refund.
> Plain language: Everything you buy comes with an automatic warranty — even without a written guarantee. Defective goods within 6 months = your choice of repair, replace, or refund.
