# Prevention of Illegal Eviction from and Unlawful Occupation of Land Act 19 of 1998 (PIE Act) — Key Sections

## Section 4 — Eviction of unlawful occupiers by owner or person in charge
(1) An owner or person in charge of land may institute proceedings for the eviction of an unlawful occupier.
(2) The court must consider all relevant circumstances including the rights of the elderly, children, disabled persons, and households headed by women.
(6) If an unlawful occupier has occupied the land for more than six months, the court must consider whether land has been made available by the municipality for relocation.
> Plain language: Even if you're occupying land illegally, you can only be evicted through a court process that considers your circumstances.

## Section 6 — Prohibition on illegal eviction
No person may evict an unlawful occupier except on the authority of an order of a competent court.
> Plain language: ALL evictions require a court order. Self-help evictions (changing locks, removing belongings, cutting services) are ILLEGAL.

## Section 8 — Offence
Any person who evicts or causes an eviction without a court order is guilty of an offence and liable on conviction to a fine or imprisonment for a period not exceeding two years.
> Plain language: Illegal eviction is a CRIMINAL OFFENCE. The person who evicts you illegally can go to jail for up to 2 years.

## Key protections
- NO eviction without a court order — ever
- The court must consider: how long you've lived there, whether you have alternative accommodation, whether children/elderly/disabled people are affected
- The municipality has obligations to provide emergency housing in certain cases
- Illegal eviction is a criminal offence
- If you are being illegally evicted, call the police (10111) immediately
