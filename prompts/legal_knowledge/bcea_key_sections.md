# Basic Conditions of Employment Act 75 of 1997 — Key Sections

## Section 9 — Ordinary hours of work
(1) An employer may not require or permit an employee to work more than 45 hours in any week, and nine hours in any day if the employee works for five days or fewer in a week, or eight hours in any day if the employee works on more than five days in a week.
> Plain language: You cannot be forced to work more than 45 hours per week, or more than 9 hours per day (5-day week) / 8 hours per day (6-day week).

## Section 10 — Overtime
(1) An employer may not require or permit an employee to work overtime except by agreement.
(2) An employer may not require or permit an employee to work more than 10 hours' overtime in any week.
(3) An employer must pay an employee at least one and one-half times the employee's wage for overtime worked.
> Plain language: Overtime must be agreed to, cannot exceed 10 hours per week, and must be paid at 1.5x your normal rate.

## Section 15 — Sunday work
(1) An employer may not require an employee to work on a Sunday unless agreed.
(2) An employer must pay an employee who works on a Sunday at double the employee's normal wage for each hour worked, unless the employee ordinarily works on a Sunday, in which case at one and one-half times.
> Plain language: Sunday work must be agreed to and paid at double time (or 1.5x if you normally work Sundays).

## Section 20 — Annual leave
(1) An employee is entitled to 21 consecutive days' annual leave on full remuneration in each annual leave cycle.
(2) An employer must grant annual leave not later than six months after the end of the annual leave cycle.
> Plain language: You get at least 21 consecutive days (15 working days) of paid leave per year.

## Section 22 — Sick leave
(1) An employee is entitled to paid sick leave equal to the number of days the employee would normally work during a period of six weeks in every sick leave cycle of 36 months.
(2) During the first six months of employment, an employee is entitled to one day's paid sick leave for every 26 days worked.
> Plain language: Over a 3-year cycle, you get about 30-36 days of paid sick leave (depending on your work schedule).

## Section 25 — Maternity leave
(1) An employee is entitled to at least four consecutive months of maternity leave.
(3) No employee may work for six weeks after the birth of her child, unless a medical practitioner or midwife certifies that she is fit to do so.
> Plain language: You get 4 months maternity leave. You must take at least 6 weeks off after giving birth.

## Section 35 — Notice of termination
(1) A contract of employment terminable at the instance of a party to the contract may be terminated only on notice of not less than—
(a) one week, if the employee has been employed for six months or less;
(b) two weeks, if the employee has been employed for more than six months but not more than one year;
(c) four weeks, if the employee has been employed for one year or more.
> Plain language: Notice periods: 1 week (under 6 months), 2 weeks (6-12 months), 4 weeks (over 1 year).

## National Minimum Wage Act (2018)
The national minimum wage is reviewed annually. Employers must pay at least the gazetted minimum wage per hour.
> Plain language: There is a legal minimum wage that all employers must pay. Check the current rate as it is updated annually.
