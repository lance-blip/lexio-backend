# Rental Housing Act 50 of 1999 — Key Sections

## Section 4 — Rights of tenants
(1) A tenant has the right to—
(a) have the dwelling maintained in a condition fit for habitation;
(b) privacy — the landlord must give reasonable notice before entering;
(c) not have services (water, electricity) cut off by the landlord as a means of enforcement;
(d) receive a written lease agreement;
(e) have their deposit held in an interest-bearing account.
> Plain language: Your landlord must keep the property livable, respect your privacy, cannot cut your water/electricity, and must give you a written lease.

## Section 4(5) — Deposit protection
A landlord must invest the tenant's deposit in an interest-bearing account with a financial institution and provide the tenant with written proof.
> Plain language: Your deposit must earn interest in a bank account. The landlord must show you proof.

## Section 4(6) — Deposit refund
The landlord must refund the deposit plus interest within 14 days of the lease ending, minus any amounts lawfully owed. The landlord must provide an itemised list of deductions.
> Plain language: After you move out, your deposit + interest must be returned within 14 days. Any deductions must be listed and justified.

## Section 5 — Obligations of tenants
A tenant must pay rent on time, maintain the property in a reasonable condition, and not use the property for illegal purposes.
> Plain language: Pay your rent on time, take care of the property, and don't use it for illegal activities.

## Section 5(3) — Rent increases
A landlord may increase rent with reasonable notice and the increase must not be unreasonable. What is "reasonable" depends on circumstances.
> Plain language: Rent increases must be reasonable and you must be given proper notice. Excessive increases can be challenged.

## Section 13 — Rental Housing Tribunals
Tenants and landlords can approach the Rental Housing Tribunal in their province to resolve disputes without going to court. This service is free.
> Plain language: If you have a dispute with your landlord, you can go to the Rental Housing Tribunal for FREE — no lawyers needed.

## Key tenant protections
- A landlord CANNOT change locks or evict you without a court order
- A landlord CANNOT cut off water or electricity to force you out
- A landlord CANNOT enter your home without reasonable notice
- A verbal lease is legally binding — you don't need a written one for it to be valid
- Deposits must be returned within 14 days of lease ending
